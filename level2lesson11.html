Seasons, Months, Days and Colors

The Seasons

In English, generally speaking we have 4 main seasons. Winter, Summer, Fall and Spring. Now depending on where you live in the world- you might experience the seasons differently. If you dont know already, Ceron is a rather cold to temperate country and so the winters can be very long. Because of this, we have many words for the season 'winter' and not so many words for the other seasons. Due to English becoming a global language and to decrease confusion, many of the tradtional words for winter have disappeared in Ceron. Despite this though, some words unique to Ceron still remain. Here is a list of the main season words in Ceron along with their rough description:

aula= Summer

Summer (This word describes roughly the English months May to September and so is often translated as 'summer'. This word literaly means before harvest of pumpkins.)

Aula ae aoud a= Summer is so hot

Au aula a mahare a bouda dean= You should drink water in the summer

Aula ae sharon a= Summer is so nice

There is no word for fall in Ceron.

Helron= Winter

This word describes the onest of winter when we transition from summer to winter and the days become shorter. This word literally means 'Light no more'. This word is translated as "Winter" in English although this word is seldom used to describe what English people think 'winter' is. We most often use this word to describe the months of Novemember to the first week of January.

Shadar= Winter

This word literally means 'dark days' and is only used during the middle of winter/the peak of winter when the days are shortest. Nowadays (by younger people) the word is used during the whole duration of winter as many people like how the word sounds. In terms of correctness though, this word should only be used seldomly and sometimes can only be used for a few days. The Shadar festival (Shadarbedna) usally denotes the beginning of the season.

Helron ae fward gla= Winter was so cold

E shadar a etala ra sa= I will go to italy in the winter

Shadar sharona ra= Winter was nice

E shadar man= I hate winter

----

Aushar= Winter

This word denotes the end of winter and is only used during the last days. This word can also be used to describe the beginning of spring. (Or spring as a whole)

Aushar= Spring

Boom= Spring

Spring (This word roughly describes the English months March to May and is a loan word. Many people prefer to refer to spring as 'aushar'.)

Aushar mene sharon a genbar a= Spring is my favourite season

Aushar paremous a naren somer= Spring has the perfect weather

An boom a reuron a sa raaaren= She is going to travel to Japan in the spring

Well that concludes the main seasons in Ceron, now, onto the names of the months. Now just like with the seasons, Ceronec months dont exactly match with the English ones. For the sake of simplicity, I'll only list the most common months used:

Celea= July, August, September

Felea= October, November

Helvea= December, January

Ulea= March, April, May, June

So these are the general 'months' in Ceron. As you can see, these words represent many different months in English. Unfortuntely, in Ceron we have a few words that we use two refer to a specific month. Those specific months are:

Co er= March

Lar er= April

We never use the word "Ulea" to refer to March or April but we do use "Ulea" to refer to June and May. Also, when refering to what month it is, we dont use the verb 'to be' instead we use this verb:

Dunea= Is the month

Examples of that in a sentence:

Co er dunea= It's March

Helvea dunea= It's December

Now lets move onto the days of the week in Ceron which are as follows. Before that though, Its important to know that in Ceron we have two different ways to call the days of the week. Basically in Ceron, (Read about Ceronec history to know more) we have Native-Words and Essonan Words. Native-Words are words that come from the Ceronec Peninsula whereas Essonan words are words that come from the neighbouring country of Essona. We use Essonan days of the week words when we say sentences like "It is Saturday" or "Tommorow is Saturday" but we use Native-Words when we say sentences like "On Saturday.." or "What time on Saturday...".

Native-Words

Abardare= Monday

Edamdare= Tuesday

Leendare= Wednesday

Mandare= Thursday

Mardare= Friday

Orendare= Saturday

Shadare= Sunday

Essonan-Words

Mardare= Monday

Theondare= Tuesday

Helondare= Wednesday

Celondare= Thursday

Fredare= Friday

Sandare= Saturday

Grodare= Sunday

(Often times these words are shortned down to just their beginning without the 'dare' at the end)

Examples of these words in sentences:

Sandare a = It's Saturday

E orendare a aud maremon ra sa= Im going to go to your house on Saturday

E abaredare man= I hate Mondays

The last topic I am going to introduce is colors. Colors in Ceron are very simple to understand and all colors in Ceron are considered NP adjectives. Funny enough, the word for 'colors' in Ceron comes from English because originally Ceron had no word do describe colors as a whole. The main colors in Ceron are as follows:

Shador a= Black

E a= White

Dargad a= Red

Feub a= Blue

Ogar a= Green

Geren a= Yellow

Uran a= Purple

Larenda a= Purple

Orange a= Orange

Solar= Color

EX

orange a gon= Orange fruit

e ogar a maremon a= I am at the green house

Than dargad a achen a somer= He has red hair (LIT: He carries red hair)

E uran a denare a sa burunburun= Im kissing the purple man (Idiom, purple means 'shy')
