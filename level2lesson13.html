Because, Also, But, Now etc

Because

Because is an extremely common word in English and is used to express 'for the reason that' or 'since'. Just like many other connecting words in Ceron, because in English cannot be translated into just one word. Instead, its translated into 3 different words whose uses differ depending on the type of words its surrounded by. Before I introduce the words for because in Ceron, look at these English sentences:

I hate you because you are mean

Im running because I need to go home

See how in all these sentences, the 'reason' comes after because. In Ceron, its the opposite. In Ceron, because is always followed by said reason and since this is the opposite of the English version it might be difficult to think about naturally. For now, think of 'because' in Ceron as 'because of this,':

You are mean BECAUSE OF THIS, I hate you

I need to go home BECAUSE OF THIS, Im running

So without further or do, here is the first type of 'because' in Ceron:

orse= Because

rse= Because (After vowels)

Now here is where we get into the nuances of the Ceronec language. This specific 'because' is placed directly after the 'reason' and is not added onto words (Unless the reason ends with a vowel). It is only used in imperative sentences (unless in a negatively connotated sentence), meaning that its used in sentences where you are requesting someone to do something. It is also used to make suggestions. (In Ceron we dont differentiate between demands and suggestions):

Oren searse au orenuarn a sare= Because it's raining, you should get an umbrella

Aoud orse au ones a thana= Because it's hot, you should take off your clothes

Even though this form is considered to be a 'suggestion' its more of a demand and can sometimes come off as a little bit rude or scary sounding. Another unique feature of this specific type of 'because' is that it only functions in the present tense, meaning that you cannot use 'orse' in the past or future. Another nuance of this 'because' is that you will find it being used in non-imperative sentences when there is a 'negative' meaning. Such as:

Au harem orse man= I hate you because you're ugly

(Notice how the pronoun is often emitted in sentences where it is extremely obvious who the subject is.) The next type of 'because' is this:

ca a= Because

This type of because is extremely simple to conjugate and understand. 'Ca a' can be used in both past and future as well as the present tense although you cannot use it in an imperative sentence. This form is usally used in plain sentences as well as sentences that have a positive nuance. (Is often used to create compliments). To further understand this, look at these examples:

Cana ca a au a sa glaman= I hated you because you were mean

(This sentence has a negative-meaning but since 'orse' cannot be used in the past tense, 'ca a' has to be used.)

Au lurenpa a somer ca a audch= Im starring at you because you are so muscular

(LIT: Im starring at you because you carry alot of muscles)

You could not say this:

Lurenpa a somer orse a audch= Im starring at you because you are so muscular

This would not be a compliment and would come off as you are starring at him because he has so much muscles (and you think that is gross and disgusting.) It would also mean "Because Im starring at you, you are so muscular" (in a question tone) which would make no sense. Aside from serving as compliments, 'ca a' can also just serve as a plain ol' because:

Ae aoud ca a maremon a sa= Im going home because its so hot

Sharon ca a remsa= I want to go because its pretty

E a sa thanmare shadera ca a erare= Im leaving because my boyfriend ditched me

(This sentence is an example where 'orse' would fit better although as a non-native speaker I know its hard to identify when a sentence might be considered negative or not. Even though 'ditched' in English is in the past tense, in Ceron it is not so 'orse' can be placed here.)

E a sa thanmare shaderarse erare= Im leaving because my boyfriend ditched me

Now the last and final type of 'because' in Ceron is this:

raeren= Because (of this) couldnt...

This is probably my favourite type of 'because' (Yes I have a favourite type of because lol...). It is attached directly onto a word and is used when the whole previous statement beforehand is an excuse or the reason why you couldnt do the next statement. You dont need to add the negative 'ha' or 'ba' when using this because the negative is built into the word. (Although sometimes modara is used alongside.) To understand this, here are some examples:

E sala a gonrunraeren unura ra= I think that I couldnt hangout because I was studying for the exam

Fraen a deremraeren glerare modara= I couldnt leave because the door was locked

Haberon a ae runeraeren edase= I cant think because it's so loud in the library

E bushraeren raunur= I wont be able to hangout with you because I'm busy

Eras bechensomerraeren a caumen modara= They cant run because they are tired

Now before I finish this up, I want to mention that 'raeren' can also have the meaning of 'in order to' in English when accompanied with either to come or to go. Here are some examples of that:

E au unuraeren glasea= I came in order to see you

Haberon a gonrunraeren a sa= I'm going in order to study at the library

Some last mintue things which arent related to this. Oftentimes you might see the past tense conjugation 'a ra' attached to both P and NP verbs and have the meaning of 'just' or the meaning of a plain past tense. This is because over time the meaning of 'a ra' has become more similar to that of 'gl/gla'. What Im trying to say is, it doesnt matter if you use 'gl' or 'a ra' for the past tense.

Also, But

Unlike other words in Ceron, 'also' and 'but' only have one translation in Ceron:

nagaer= Also

amen= But (When last letter is a consonant)

men= But (When last letter is a vowel)

We can use these words just like how we would use them in English:

Than nagaer denare a= He is also a man

E englesh nagaer a burden= I also speak English

Sometimes you might see 'nagaer' shortened down to 'na'. Here is 'but' in some sentences:

E gala somer amen faou sharon= I have a dog but I like cats

Eras haberon a amen nare sacon a= They are at the library but we are at school

Au thand renp comadhe a dean amen a ones udaerd= You can see his body but he is wearing clothes

E au a sa aesa amen mene dada ra dacde= I love you but my dad said no

(When a word ends with 'a' you must use 'amen' even though its a vowel.)

Now

Expressing 'now' in Ceron is pretty simple. In order to create sentences like "Im watching TV now" or "I want to go home now" all you need to use is this word:

Gerun= Now

So actually this word really means 'now and not any other time' and kind of gives off the vibe that youre doing this thing for the first time in your life. It also just really places an emphasis on 'now':

E gerun THV a comadhe= Im watching TV right now (Emphasis on NOW)

E gerun maremon a= Im at home right now (Emphasis that your nowhere else, youre at home)

E gerun peronmarde a sa= Right now I'm going to the corner store (Emphasis on now and corner store)

Note that when you ask a question that uses 'now' you have to use a different form of 'geren' which is:

gaer= Now?

Au gaer erare na= You are leaving right now?

Au gaer rona comadhe na= Are you watching a movie right now?

Au gaer a batare na= Are you eating rn?
