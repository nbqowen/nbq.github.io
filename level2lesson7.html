    • Pronouns and the Possessive Particle

    This is more of a snippet rather than a full lesson since in Ceron the possessive particle is not that important. In Ceron, we denote possessiveness (of pronouns) by adding -u to the end of the word. For example, here are all the main pronouns in their possessive form

    Thanu

    His

    U

    Your

    Anu

    Her

    Ne (Irregular)

    My

    Erasu

    Their

    Naru

    Our

    Now this only works with pronouns, so what would you do if you wanted to say “The house’s” or “The dog’s”? Well, you don't. If ever you would want to express a sentence like this

    The dog's house

    You would just say

    Gala mamon

    LIT : Dog house, Dog's house

    You can also add “-u” after normal nouns although this is far less common and seems kind of text-book like. If said noun ends with a vowel, remove it, and then add u.

    Galu mamon

    Dog's house

    Denu gala

    Person's dog

    The usage of “u” can also represent what is “of” in English. For example,

    All the sounds of the ocean

    Maru erac

    LIT : Ocean sound's-all
